This paper examines whether a structural model of decentralized finance (DeFi) lending can explain observed stablecoin interest rate dynamics. Using daily data from Aave, I first document reduced-form links between U.S. risk-free rates and DeFi lending rates. I then evaluate the static equilibrium model of Barbon et al. (2025) using the Simulated Method of Moments. The reduced-form evidence shows a consistent relationship between monetary policy rates and on-chain lending conditions. However, the structural model cannot replicate this pattern or other basic features of the data, and key parameters are weakly identified. These failures point to missing mechanisms, particularly dynamics, heterogeneous liquidity providers, and frictions. The results suggest that richer, explicitly dynamic models are needed before DeFi lending can be used for credible structural interpretation or policy analysis.
